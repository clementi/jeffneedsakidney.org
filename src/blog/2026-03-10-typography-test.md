"This is a line with quotation marks and the line's got an apostrophe, too."

Em-dashes work---that we know. And en-dashes work also, from 2026--. "'Nested' quotes?" They seem fine, though the spacing is sub-optimal.

We already know that equations work well:
$$
\pi = 4 - \frac{4}{3!} - \frac{12}{5!} - \frac{180}{7!} - \frac{6300}{9!} - \frac{396900}{11!} - \cdots = \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{-4((2n - 3)!!)^2}{(2n - 3)(2n - 1)!}.
$$

Inline math also: $(x_1 + x_2) + (x_1 x_2) = c$.
